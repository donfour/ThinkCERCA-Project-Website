Introduction

For our capstone project, we are collaborating with ThinkCERCA to develop a solution that assists teachers to grade and comment on students' argumentative essays more efficiently and effectively with machine augmentation.
ThinkCERCA is a Chicago-based education company aiming to advance K-12 students’ reading and writing skills. ThinkCERCA’s digital platform contains writing curriculum in English Language Arts, Social Studies, Science, and Math surrounding the CERCA framework.

CERCA is an acronym for Claim, Evidence, Reason, Counterclaim, Audience. The CERCA Framework is a scaffolding approach to literacy that helps students develop their critical thinking skills.

What are we doing?

Tech: To experiment and develop machine learning models to identify argumentative writing components as well as predict scores for different rubric items.

Design: To implement machine augmentation techniques into current grading and comment-given system.

The 98% Edcat Team

We are 98% Ed“cat”, a team of students from the METALS program at Carnegie Mellon University with various backgrounds and skills.

Primary Research

To gain more first-hand knowledge in the domain of argumentative writing grading, we conducted extensive primary research with the following methods:

  • 11 Interviews with ThinkCERCA teachers, ELA writing teachers and technology experts
  • 2 Think-aloud studies for analyzing detailed scoring and feedback giving process for argumentative writings
  • 10+ Machine learning models for predicting scores and identifying CERCA components
  • Data analysis on ThinkCERCA’s database to identify patterns
  • Product experience analysis for the ThinkCERCA platforms using role-playing technique for a deep understanding of users

Secondary Research

To complement our primary research, we also conducted secondary research with the following methods:

  • 80+ Research papers in 3 domains: pedagogy, technology, and product
  • 10+ Competitors ranging from AI scoring to AI feedback products
  • 3 Recordings observations provided by ThinkCERCA research group showing the ThinkCERCA beta version user experience as well as teachers’ scoring and feedback giving process

Ideation

We brainstormed 6 ideas and evaluated them with client’s feedback, feasibility, and impact. We decided to further develop on the idea of highlighting different rubric components to speed up the grading and commenting.

Design Goals

P1:

  • In what ways can teachers use essays highlighted by AI most effectively in scoring?
  • In what ways can teachers use essays highlighted by AI most effectively in commenting with pre-selected comments?


P2:
  • If we were to implement student highlighting (the student highlights the essay themselves), how would teachers respond to the highlights?
Beginning with rough sketches, we discussed with clients on the desired functions and crucial considerations to keep in mind.

Prototyping & Testing

Then we quickly move to wireframe prototypes to evaluate the user flow.
During multiple rounds of rapid prototyping, user testing and iteration, we consolidated feedback from ThinkCERCA users, clients, and other designers.

Tech

After reviewing relevant research papers, examining available data, and consulting experts in the field, we dedicated our time to developing two types of machine learning models - the claim score prediction model, and the claim identification model.
